# Mithra Build Week video script

**Target runtime:** 2:40. No music. Narration uses the macOS Samantha voice at
138 words per minute. Captions are burned into the composition from
`tools/demo-video/public/captions.json`.

## 0:00–0:15 — Problem and proposition

Busy couples keep money, health records, household plans, and family
responsibilities in different spreadsheets, apps, and messages. Mithra is a
family operating system that brings those facts together, then turns them into
a calm weekly plan.

## 0:15–0:35 — Product overview

The Family Brief gives the household one shared view. Finance tracks recorded
totals and trends. Health keeps reports and measurements together, without
medical advice. Planning holds dates and preparation. Each adult can keep
selected records only for themselves.

## 0:35–1:25 — Week in Review

Week in Review starts with a shared status: mostly on track. It ranks no more
than three priorities from valid shared records. This week: travel documents,
insurance renewal, and August household planning. The insurance reminder and
payment due date form one real-world event. Mithra groups only strong matches,
so it appears once. Its observation keeps the maths factual: groceries have
used 14,500 of the recorded 18,000 budget, leaving 3,500, while total spending
is lower than the same point last month. The result is a bounded next step, not
an invented recommendation. Progress shows a real change. The compact timeline
keeps the next seven days ahead of later dates. Empty sections stay hidden, so
the review stays short.

## 1:25–1:50 — Privacy and health safety

Mithra keeps private information private. The owner can see a glucose
comparison and one unit-correction notice. It names the measurement, preserves
the recorded units, and makes no medical claim. In the partner view, that
health issue does not change the shared status, summary, or empty states. Each
adult sees only their private information; household facts remain shared.

## 1:50–2:25 — Codex and GPT-5.6 collaboration

Codex inspected Mithra’s Go and SQLite app, traced the Family Brief and Week
paths, then added Week-specific handling for finance, planning, and health.
Codex added tests, privacy checks, browser verification, and this Remotion
demo. GPT-5.6 supported product critique, UX choices, prioritisation, prompt
design, and review. The builder made each scope, privacy, safety, and product
decision. Mithra calculates facts from evidence; optional AI can interpret
them, never invent or change records.

## 2:25–2:40 — Closing

Mithra helps busy couples see what changed, prepare for what is next, and keep
private facts private. The hosted demo and public repository are ready to
explore.
