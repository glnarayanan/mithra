Help

Use Mithra with the facts in view.

Start here

Use Capture for an update in your own words, or Import for an existing CSV, Excel, or PDF file. Mithra keeps each resulting record linked to its source so you can check it.

Press Ctrl+K or Command+K to open quick navigation.

Shared and Only you

Shared records are visible to both active adults. Only you records are visible only to you and never shape your partner’s Family Brief or Week in Review. Choose the visibility that fits each Capture or Import.

Records

Finance shows exact totals and recorded changes, not financial advice. Health keeps reported values and dates together without diagnosis, clinical interpretation, or treatment recommendations. Planning keeps household dates and plans connected; calendar exports open one-time drafts for your review and never sync in the background.

Family Brief and Week in Review

Your Family Brief and Week in Review summarize the records and evidence visible to you. Shared and Only you wording are prepared separately. Mithra does not decide, diagnose, mediate, or judge for either partner.

OpenAI boundary

OpenAI is optional and runs only after you request an AI action. Mithra sends only the material needed for that action, asks OpenAI not to store responses, and never lets OpenAI make changes on its own. You can connect or disconnect the household key in Settings.

Visual PDF transfer

Mithra reads PDFs locally first. If a PDF has no recoverable text, it asks for one explicit confirmation before sending that one encrypted source to OpenAI to read visible pages. Cancelling sends no file and deletes the staged source.

Health corrections

If values cannot be compared safely, Mithra keeps them separate until you enter a corrected value and unit. The original source remains linked; Mithra does not guess a conversion.

Calendar exports

Downloading an .ics file or opening a Google Calendar draft creates a one-time export. Later Mithra changes do not update another calendar.

Deleting a source and recovery

Deleting a source makes it and its dependent live records and pending work inaccessible together. If encrypted-file cleanup is delayed, Mithra retries it safely after recovery. A deleted source is not available from Mithra again.
